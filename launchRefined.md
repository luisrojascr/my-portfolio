## The gap between “it works” and “it feels ready”

Most AI-generated apps are functional enough to demo, but still fall short in the places that matter most once real users arrive.

### Generic UX
The app technically works, but the experience feels templated, inconsistent, or forgettable.

### Weak product hierarchy
Important actions do not stand out, screens compete for attention, and users are left guessing what matters.

### Trust friction
Small details signal risk: awkward spacing, inconsistent copy, broken states, and flows that feel unfinished.

### Conversion leakage
Users drop off because the product feels unclear, weak, slow, or not credible enough.

### Performance drag
Slow loads, laggy interactions, and clunky mobile behavior make the product feel fragile and reduce confidence fast.

---

## Refinement, not a rebuild

You do not always need to throw away the AI-generated version and start over. In many cases, the fastest path is to keep what is already working and improve the parts that affect perception, usability, speed, and conversion.

### UI polish
Sharpen spacing, hierarchy, visual consistency, and states so the product feels designed instead of assembled.

### UX clarity
Make flows easier to follow, reduce hesitation, and help users understand what to do next.

### Flow optimization
Remove unnecessary friction from onboarding, form experiences, and key conversion paths.

### Performance and responsiveness
Improve perceived speed, interaction smoothness, and responsiveness in the moments that affect trust most.

### Production-minded cleanup
Address edge cases, rough interactions, loading states, and the details that break trust during real usage.
